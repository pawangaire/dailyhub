# DailyHub Phased Roadmap

This roadmap is distilled from the DailyHub research paper. It keeps the project modular so the platform can be built step by step instead of trying to launch every service at once.

## Phase 1: Foundation

Build the shared platform base.

- User registration and login
- Role-based accounts
- Profile management
- Location system
- Basic search
- Notification foundation
- Admin access

## Phase 2: Service Marketplace MVP

Launch the first usable customer-facing module.

- Service categories
- Service provider profiles
- Provider discovery by location
- Booking requests
- Booking status updates
- Customer reviews and ratings
- Basic provider verification

## Phase 3: Admin and Safety

Strengthen platform trust before expanding into more complex modules.

- Provider approval workflows
- User reports
- Listing moderation
- Review moderation
- User suspension controls
- Dispute records

## Phase 4: Job Platform

Add hiring and work discovery after the core trust layer is stable.

- Job posts
- Job applications
- Worker profiles
- Employer profiles
- Safety reporting

## Phase 5: Buy-and-Sell Marketplace

Add local item listings and buyer-seller communication.

- Product listings
- Listing images
- Categories
- Location-based listings
- Buyer-seller chat
- Scam reporting

## Phase 6: Delivery System

Add delivery only after the platform has enough operational maturity.

- Parcel requests
- Food and grocery delivery flows
- Rider assignment
- Delivery tracking
- Delivery fee calculation
- Proof of delivery

## Phase 7: Business Dashboard

Give local shops and businesses a dedicated presence.

- Business profiles
- Service or product display
- Customer contact options
- Business verification
- Basic analytics

## Phase 8: Digital Payments

Introduce payments in stages, after core marketplace behavior is trusted.

- Cash and manual payment records
- Wallet and card integration
- Payment status tracking
- Escrow for high-trust transactions
- Refund and dispute support

## Early Build Rule

Keep payments, delivery, and escrow out of the first milestone. They add operational and trust complexity that should come after the core service marketplace is working.
